[[Indonesia|1]], [[Nigeria|2]] increase relations for world peace
[[REPUBLIKA.CO.ID|3]], [[JAKARTA|4]] - [[Foreign|5]] minister [[Marty Natalegawa|6]] said [[Indonesia|1]] [[and|7]] [[Nigeria|2]] would strengthen their bilateral relations to contribute to world peace [[and|7]] security. 
"[[Indonesia|1]] [[and|7]] [[Nigeria|2]] would strengthen each other not only for the [[sake|8]] of bilateral relations but also to contribute to international peace [[and|7]] security," he said after a Joint Commission Meeting at his office on Thursday.
The meeting on Thursday [[led|9]] jointly by Minister [[Marty Natalegawa|6]] [[and|7]] his Nigerian counterpart Olugbenga Ashiru was held to discuss the follow up of bilateral commitments made during the state visit of President [[Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono|10]] on February 2-3 in this year. The commitments include increasing the two countries` bilateral relations in various fields including politics [[and|7]] security, trade, investment [[and|7]] social-culture. 
Natalegawa said relations between the two countries had gone long [[and|7]] well so that wider opportunities were open for the two countries to increase cooperation in the future. He [[cited|11]] [[Nigeria|2]] was an important country in Africa. 
"[[Nigeria|2]] is a strong partner for [[Indonesia|1]]," he added.
[[Nigeria`s|2]] Minister of [[Foreign Affairs|12]] Olugbenga Ashiru meanwhile said he was glad for the implementation of the Joint Commission Meeting. "The two countries` agreement on the commission is a momentum for increasing cooperation between [[Nigeria|2]] [[and|7]] [[Indonesia|1]]," he said.
[[Indonesia|1]] [[and|7]] [[Nigeria|2]] held a Joint Commission Meeting to follow up their agreement on the establishment of the commission for the cooperation between the two countries [[signed in|13]] [[New York|14]] in September 2010.
The bilateral relations between [[Nigeria|2]] [[and|7]] [[Indonesia|1]] have been established since [[1965|15]] marked by the opening of a [[diplomatic|16]] relations [[and|7]] the Indonesian embassy in [[Lagos|17]], which was later moved to [[Abuja|17]] in 2008. [[Nigeria|2]] opened its embassy in [[Jakarta|4]] in [[1976|18]].
